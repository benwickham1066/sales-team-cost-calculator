How the number builds

How this is calculated — and what's research vs judgement

Top line (revenue at risk). Gallup's Q12 meta-analysis finds a 20% gap in sales between top- and bottom-quartile engagement units, and that managers account for ~70% of engagement variance (State of the American Manager, 2015). We never apply the full gap: the observed-pattern score positions you on a conservative band that tops out at half of it — a ceiling of ~7% of quota, lower the fewer behaviours ticked.

The bridge is ours. Linking a reactive pattern to lower engagement is SSC's reasoning, not Gallup's — which is why this is a modelled estimate, with every assumption visible.

Bottom line (churn & ramp). Only departures above your normal turnover count, only ~70% attributed to the leader, and scaled by the observed pattern. Each costs a replacement (a % of OTE) plus the quota a ramping seat can't yet carry. Industry benchmarks you can adjust, not research constants.

No double-counting. For a sales team the productivity loss is the revenue gap, so it's priced once — on the top line — not stacked twice.
