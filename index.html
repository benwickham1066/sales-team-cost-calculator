How the number builds

How this is calculated — and what's research vs judgement

Top line (revenue at risk). Gallup's Q12 meta-analysis finds a 20% gap in sales between top- and bottom-quartile engagement units, and that managers account for ~70% of engagement variance (State of the American Manager, 2015). We never apply the full gap: the observed-pattern score positions you on a conservative band that tops out at half that gap. So the ceiling on any team is ~7% of quota — and it's lower the fewer behaviours you tick.

The bridge is ours. Linking a reactive pattern to lower engagement is SSC's join, not Gallup's — Leadership Circle measures reactive tendency, Gallup measures engagement. It's a reasoned assumption, which is why this is a modelled estimate.

Bottom line (churn & ramp). Only departures above your normal turnover are counted, and only ~70% attributed to the leader. Each costs a replacement (a % of OTE) plus the quota a ramping seat can't carry. These are industry benchmarks you can adjust, not research constants.

No double-counting. For a sales team the productivity loss is the revenue gap, so we price it once (top line) rather than stacking a separate productivity figure on top.
